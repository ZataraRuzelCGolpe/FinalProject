Our marine ecosystems play indispensable roles in sustaining nature, humanity, and our planet. They offer essential services such as regulating Earth's temperature by absorbing excess heat, storing and purifying freshwater, providing natural infrastructure to protect from storms and erosion, and generating more than half of the oxygen we breathe.

However, the health of these ecosystems faces a grave threat from the triple planetary crisis and other pressures such as overfishing, pollution, and unsustainable development. With that, we must be educated on the importance of maintaining the health of marine ecosystems so that they can continue thriving and because we also benefit from them.




It is evident from all the articles that organizations such as UNEP, NOAA, WWF, and etc. publish, that our oceans are not at their best conditions with increasing human activity that emits plenty of toxic wastes that harm these blue ecosystems.
