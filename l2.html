Overview

Sustainable marine development is essential for preserving ocean ecosystems, but various threats jeopardize this goal. In this page, we will be showing the different threats that marine development is exposed to. Most of these are human-induced as we participate in activities that result to these.

Major Threats

Overfishing

Depletes fish populations and disrupts marine food chains.

Pollution

Chemicals and plastics harm marine life and habitats.

Climate Change

Alters ocean temperatures and acidifies waters, affecting biodiversity.

Habitat Destruction

Coastal development and bottom trawling destroy critical marine habitats.

Conclusion

Marine Development is threatened by many different factors. These include overfishing, pollution, climate change, habitat destruction and plenty more. Addressing these threats is crucial for the health of our oceans and the future of marine resources. If we let these problems worsen, we will lose a significant amount of resources and hinder progress.
